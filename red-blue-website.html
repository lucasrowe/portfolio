Overview

Designed and built websites to support Red Blue Games company pages, including company services and landing pages for individual games.

Company Website

Responsive landing page and children pages for company information, services, and lightweight teases of each of our products.

Audience: Partner companies & game publishers Goal: Convert to product sales or partnerships Design Aesthetic: Fun and credible Tech Stack: Bootstrap, JQuery, Github Pages (Jekyll and Liquid)

Red Blue Games launched small minigames, each taking an aggressive, one-month timeline to create. To do this we took advantage of in-house created UX templates both in the games and on the website. The web used Liquid and Jekyll to create responsive, lightweight landing pages for each of our minigames. This allowed us to generate websites with little overhead.

Minigame Landing Pages

Each minigame received its own responsive landing page, designed to work across tablet and mobile. The pages reused the game's existing art and branding to keep production time low while still feeling polished and cohesive with the game itself.

Sparklite Landing Page

A landing page for the studio's flagship title, Sparklite, showcasing the charming art and engaging game world. It was important for this page to be memorable because press articles and marketplaces would link directly to this page.

Audience: Press and Players Goal: Convert to newsletter signups and store clicks Design Aesthetic: Rich, colorful, and engaging Tech Stack: Bootstrap, Javascript, Github Pages

We reused game art and used a dramatic parallax effect to create a sense of flying over the game world. The first wipe past the clouds reveals the dynamic and colorful game world, providing a sense of wonder. The site's minimalism also allows for the gorgeous pixel art to speak for itself.

Results

Results

The Red Blue Games websites served as the primary touchpoint for publisher outreach, press coverage, and direct consumer sales across all of the studio's titles. The design system and templating approach enabled rapid deployment of new game microsites with minimal engineering overhead — a critical capability for a small indie studio shipping multiple titles.
