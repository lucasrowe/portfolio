Sparklite
UX Design & Art Direction — Red Blue Games
Shipped November 2019
Role UX Design & Art Direction
Platform PC, Switch, Xbox, PS4, Mobile
Company Red Blue Games
Overview

Action Adventure game that retailed on all major game platforms and later was brought to iOS and Android.

The Project

Sparklite took over four years to make, with pauses in between to raise money from publishers and perform contract work for clients. After demoing the game for two years, we secured $300k in funding to complete and market the game, which allowed us to scale up our development. At its largest the core team consisted of a game engine programmer, UI programmer, game/UX designer, two gameplay programmers, and three pixel artists. Two of the roles I filled on the project were Art Director and UX Designer for game interfaces.

Storyboarding

Many of our scripted story sequences were storyboarded in Figma to get a sense for how the scene will feel. Staying low-fidelity allows us to course-correct before committing resources to art, engineering, and audio.

UX Design

Game menus and complicated user interactions were mocked up in Figma prototypes. These were used to eliminate UX issues before investing in programming but also served as requirements for engineers. This was especially valuable for novel UX interactions that couldn't be referenced in other games.

Art Direction

One of my many roles throughout the production was providing Art Direction to our animators, environment artists, and illustrators. Aside from the game art, we also produced retail goods that required art direction. We also directed PR firms for trailer production.

We were fortunate to work with some of the best pixel artists in the industry and it was a privilege to lead this talented team. The sketches below are contributions from the team under my direction.

Results

Results

Sparklite shipped globally across all major platforms in November 2019. It was well-reviewed, receiving a 75 Metacritic score on Switch and received a Unity3D nomination for Best 2D Visuals. It was subsequently released to iOS and Android and received Best Foreign Mobile Game in the French Pégases 2022 Awards and has over a 4.5 star rating on both stores.
